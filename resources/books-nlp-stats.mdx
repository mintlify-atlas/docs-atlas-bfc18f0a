This page covers the essential reading for NLP practitioners and anyone building a rigorous mathematical foundation for machine learning. The math sections are particularly relevant for understanding why ML algorithms work, not just how to run them.

## Natural language processing

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="NLTK Book" icon="language" href="https://www.nltk.org/book/">
    Natural Language Processing with Python. The standard hands-on introduction to NLP using the NLTK library. Covers text classification, tagging, parsing, and semantic analysis. Free online.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Foundations of Statistical NLP" icon="language" href="https://nlp.stanford.edu/fsnlp/promo/">
    Manning and Schütze's thorough statistical treatment of natural language processing. Covers n-gram models, part-of-speech tagging, parsing, and information extraction.
  </Card>
</Columns>

- [Coursera Course Book on NLP](http://www.cs.columbia.edu/~mcollins/notes-spring2013.html) — Michael Collins's NLP lecture notes from Columbia. Free online.
- [Natural Language Processing in Action](https://www.manning.com/books/natural-language-processing-in-action) — Practical NLP with Python. Covers tokenization, embeddings, topic modeling, and sequence-to-sequence models.
- [Natural Language Processing in Action, Second Edition](https://www.manning.com/books/natural-language-processing-in-action-second-edition) — Updated edition covering transformers, BERT, and modern NLP pipelines.
- [Real-World Natural Language Processing](https://www.manning.com/books/real-world-natural-language-processing) — Building production NLP applications with Python.
- [Essential Natural Language Processing](https://www.manning.com/books/essential-natural-language-processing) — A focused guide to the key concepts and tools for NLP practitioners.
- [Deep Learning for Natural Language Processing](https://www.manning.com/books/deep-learning-for-natural-language-processing) — Applying deep learning methods specifically to NLP tasks.
- [Getting Started with Natural Language Processing](https://www.manning.com/books/getting-started-with-natural-language-processing) — Entry-level guide for readers new to NLP.
- [Transfer Learning for Natural Language Processing](https://www.manning.com/books/transfer-learning-for-natural-language-processing) — Paul Azunre. Using pretrained models and fine-tuning for downstream NLP tasks.

## Probability & statistics

A solid understanding of probability and statistics is foundational to nearly every ML algorithm. These books cover the subject at various levels.

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Think Stats" icon="chart-bar" href="https://www.greenteapress.com/thinkstats/">
    Allen Downey's introduction to statistics for programmers. Uses Python throughout. Covers distributions, hypothesis testing, and regression. Free online.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Introduction to Probability (MIT)" icon="chart-bar" href="http://athenasc.com/probbook.html">
    Bertsekas and Tsitsiklis. The textbook for MIT's probability course. Covers discrete and continuous distributions, Bayesian inference, Markov chains, and limit theorems.
  </Card>
</Columns>

- [Introduction to Probability (Dartmouth)](https://math.dartmouth.edu/~prob/prob/prob.pdf) — Free PDF introduction to probability theory.
- [Basic Probability Theory](https://www.math.uiuc.edu/~r-ash/BPT/BPT.pdf) — Ash's concise and rigorous treatment.
- [Probability & Statistics Cookbook](http://statistics.zone/) — A quick-reference compendium of formulas, definitions, and results.
- [Probability Theory: The Logic of Science](https://bayes.wustl.edu/etj/prob/book.pdf) — E.T. Jaynes's influential Bayesian treatment of probability as extended logic.
- [Introduction to statistical thought](https://people.math.umass.edu/~lavine/Book/book.pdf) — Lavine's introductory statistics text.
- [Introduction to Probability and Statistics Using R](http://ipsur.r-forge.r-project.org/book/download/IPSUR.pdf) — Statistical inference with R.
- [The Elements of Statistical Learning](https://web.stanford.edu/~hastie/ElemStatLearn/) — Hastie, Tibshirani, Friedman. The comprehensive graduate-level statistics and ML reference. Free PDF.
- [An Introduction to Statistical Learning with Applications in R](https://www-bcf.usc.edu/~gareth/ISL/) — Accessible applied statistics and ML with R.
- [From Algorithms to Z-Scores](http://heather.cs.ucdavis.edu/probstatbook) — Matloff's probabilistic and statistical modeling text.
- [Practical Regression and Anova using R](https://cran.r-project.org/doc/contrib/Faraway-PRA.pdf) — Julian Faraway.
- [R practicals](http://www.columbia.edu/~cjd11/charles_dimaggio/DIRE/resources/R/practicalsBookNoAns.pdf) — Statistical computing exercises using R.
- [The R Inferno](https://www.burns-stat.com/pages/Tutor/R_inferno.pdf) — Patrick Burns. Traps and pitfalls in R programming.
- [Advanced R Programming](http://adv-r.had.co.nz) — Hadley Wickham. Deep dive into R internals and functional programming patterns.
- [The Art of R Programming](http://heather.cs.ucdavis.edu/~matloff/132/NSPpart.pdf) — Norman Matloff.

## Linear algebra

Linear algebra underpins nearly all of machine learning — from matrix factorizations in recommendation systems to the weight matrices in neural networks.

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="The Matrix Cookbook" icon="table" href="https://www.math.uwaterloo.ca/~hwolkowi/matrixcookbook.pdf">
    A compact reference of matrix identities, derivatives, and results. Indispensable for researchers and practitioners who work with matrix equations regularly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Convex Optimization" icon="chart-line" href="https://web.stanford.edu/~boyd/cvxbook/bv_cvxbook.pdf">
    Boyd and Vandenberghe's authoritative text on convex optimization. Understanding convexity is essential for understanding why gradient descent works. Free PDF from Stanford.
  </Card>
</Columns>

- [Linear Algebra by Shilov](https://cosmathclub.files.wordpress.com/2014/10/georgi-shilov-linear-algebra4.pdf) — A rigorous and complete treatment of linear algebra.
- [Linear Algebra Done Wrong](https://www.math.brown.edu/~treil/papers/LADW/LADW.html) — Treil. Despite the name, a thorough and modern approach. Free online.
- [Linear Algebra, Theory, and Applications](https://math.byu.edu/~klkuttle/Linearalgebra.pdf) — A comprehensive linear algebra textbook.
- [Applied Numerical Computing](https://www.seas.ucla.edu/~vandenbe/ee133a.html) — Van den Berghe, UCLA. Numerical methods for large-scale linear systems.
- [The Math Behind Artificial Intelligence](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/the-math-behind-artificial-intelligence-book) — freeCodeCamp. Covers the linear algebra, probability, and calculus used in AI.
- [Mathematics for Machine Learning](https://mml-book.github.io/) — Deisenroth, Faisal, Ong. A self-contained text that bridges undergraduate math and ML. Free online.

## Calculus

Calculus — particularly differentiation — is the foundation of gradient-based learning. The books below range from introductory to rigorous.

- [Calculus Made Easy](https://github.com/lahorekid/Calculus/blob/master/Calculus%20Made%20Easy.pdf) — Silvanus Thompson's enduring classic. Demystifies calculus for beginners through plain language. Free PDF.
- [Calculus by Ron Larson](https://www.pdfdrive.com/calculus-e183995561.html) — A standard university-level calculus textbook covering single and multivariable calculus.
- [Active Calculus](https://scholarworks.gvsu.edu/books/20/) — Matt Boelkins. An inquiry-based open textbook. Free online and PDF.

<Tip>
  For machine learning specifically, focus on differentiation (the chain rule underlies backpropagation), multivariable calculus (gradients and partial derivatives), and Taylor series. Most practitioners do not need a full calculus sequence — a focused review of these topics is sufficient.
</Tip>
